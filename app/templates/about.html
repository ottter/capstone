Why we are here

I can't tell you how many times I've been sitting in class listening to a lecture, and for whatever reason, I lose my spot in the notes and missed something important. Whether a vocabulary term for an upcoming exam or a key line needed to understand a math algorithm, this just happens sometimes. Other times I will be at home studying and notice that I took poor notes the week before and it doesn't help me. What am I supposed to do next?

MGA Connect was created to fill this niche role by providing a hub for MGA students to go to and collaborate with their fellow classmates. By creating a database for personalized class notes, it simplifies the process for students to go and find those notes they are missing. Our mission is to provide an alternative option for students to work together where applicable to learn to the best of their ability.

Note to Professors

MGA Connect is a completely voluntary service. Professors have the option to opt-out of their having class being available on database. We have a strict policy of not allowing quiz or exam answers and we do not allow students to upload content that is not their own. Everything available on our service is completely user-generated.

Future Plans for MGA Connect

MGA Connect does not have every feature implemented in it's current state that is desired. before a full roll-out can be achieved, there are a small number of features and modifications that should be added to consider this ready for production. Several of these were considered out of scope features that go well beyond what was required for a fully functional website

  • Database Improvements - In order to handle large amounts of user traffic, a better database implementation will be required. MGA Connect currently uses SQLite and the SQLAlchemy toolkit, which is sufficient for the Capstone project, but insufficient for larger usage.
  • Server Improvements - Similar to the previous item, this website is hosted on James' personal free Heroku account and that is definitely insufficient in a proper live environment.
  • Proper Permissions - The overall vision of this website involves a resource both students and professors find valuable. I feel like proper permissions for students which only allows them access to classrooms if they are currently enrolled in said class would help fit that goal.
  • Security Measures - The live version of MGA Connect allows practically anyone to create and access the website. This is purposeful for the scope of the Capstone project, but a feature in the future would see that users signing up would have to confirm their email before an account is created. There is a possibility this feature is added to a separate branch in my GitHub repository that hosts the codebase for this website.

Many, or even all, of the listed improvements above would be solved by implementing this website in to the MGA web ecosystem. With proper access to the professor and student database tables we would not need account creations and access permissions would be much more customizable than what is in place here.
